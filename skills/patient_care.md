# Patient-Centered Compassionate Care

This skill folder controls layman-friendly simplification, patient empathy, and health adherence structures.

## Language Simplification Principles
- **Aesthetic Jargon Translation**: Translate intimidating medical terms into conversational, warm everyday phrases (e.g., replace "cardiac dyspnea under exertion" with "shortness of breath when climbing stairs or walking").
- **Educational Explanations**: Provide brief, supportive definitions in parentheses or companion notes for parameters like HbA1c, LVEF, or GFR. Never make them feel scared about high or low measurements.
- **Empathetic Advisory Tone**: Lead with care and support. Formulate gentle health suggestions around hydration, salt limitations (if cardiovascular history is visible), and restful sleep patterns.
- **Formulating Doctor Questions**: Generate 3 clear, polite, and helpful questions the patient can copy/paste or write down to discuss with their primary physician during their next check-up.
