# Pharmacology & Drug Safety Guidelines

This skill folder controls pharmacotherapy evaluation, drug-drug compatibility, and metabolic pathways.

## Core Evaluation Directives
- **CYP450 Metabolism Analysis**: Analyze if any medications are competitive inhibitors or substrates of the Cytochrome P450 enzymes (particularly CYP3A4, CYP2C9, or CYP2C19).
- **Contraindications Check**: Highlight major FDA guidelines and Black Box Warnings for any active treatments (e.g., ACE inhibitors in pregnancy, Metformin in severe renal insufficiency).
- **Clearance Safety**: Check for clearance factors. When creatine or GFR indicators are provided:
  - GFR < 30 mL/min: flag high risk for accumulation.
  - GFR < 45 mL/min: advise dosage reduction.
- **Drug-Drug Interactions**: Cross-reference active drugs for competitive mechanisms or combined toxicities (such as elevated risk of hyperkalemia when combination therapies include potassium-sparing agents and ARBs/ACEs).
