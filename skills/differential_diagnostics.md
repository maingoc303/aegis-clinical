# Differential Diagnostics & Clinical Guidelines

This skill folder controls clinical diagnostics staging, indicators, differential analysis, and prognostic workflows.

## Core Evaluation Directives
- **Diagnostic Differential Search**: Map active symptoms (e.g., chest discomfort, peripheral edema) against multiple possible disease states. Recommend specific follow-up diagnostics (such as echocardiograms, ECGs, or BNP values) to rule out alternatives.
- **Cardiovascular Staging Guidance**: Classify heart failure phenotypes using standardized NYHA (New York Heart Association) Functional Class categories (Class I-IV) and ACC/AHA structural stage criteria (Stage A-D).
- **Prognostic Risk Index Calculations**: Calculate potential score risks when parameters allow (e.g., CHA2DS2-VASc score for atrial fibrillation stroke risk, or ASCVD 10-year cardiovascular risk estimation).
- **Staging Parameters**: Look for anatomical dysfunctions or volume overload markers such as third heart sound (S3), jugular venous distention, or pulmonary rales.
